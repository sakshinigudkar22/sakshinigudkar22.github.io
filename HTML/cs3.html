Designing an immersive tour experience

Project

Passion Project

My Role

UI/UX Designer

Duration

8 weeks

Overview

While I was working on my third portfolio project for Google UX Design Course which was a Responsive Website for an NGO I realized that very less number of NGOs had an independent platform to connect with their volunteers. As I conducted user interviews, the problem became clearer. Usually the campaigns or programs conducted by NGOs were missed by it’s volunteers due to lack of information.

The volunteers too needed a way to socialize with like minded people. That’s when I decided that I am going to try to find a way through it by designing a platform for volunteers.

Design Process

I’ve presented the case study in a linear fashion but I follow a lean approach towards designing. Sometimes I brainstorm a few ideas before I’ve conducted user research. It helps me to enter the problem more naturally. Or sometimes even when I’ve completed the research and I’m still prototyping, I constantly look for any new insights from potential users. I believe the process of designing is never ending because there’s no ‘Perfect’ design.

Empathize

What motivates the user?

Online Survey

I conducted a user survey with a sample size of 14 people who were volunteers, potential volunteers, NGO staff whose age ranged from 18 to 65.

Survey Results

Survey Insights

All the participants want to be involved in volunteering activities but lack of information and time constraint are the major problems faced by them.

1:1 Interviews

In order to dig deeper into user pain points I conducted structured User Interviews. 5 Interviewee included 3 volunteers of the NGO, 1 novice volunteer & 1 core committee member of the NGO.

Key Quotes from Interview

Affinity Mapping

In order to synthesize the insights from the research I used Affinity Mapping.

Key Research Insights

Users need deeper understanding about the artwork, its interpretation and the process.

Busy individuals want to get the best experience within a time restricted duration.

Most visitors worry about the hygiene when it comes to audio devices provided by the library.

Visitors find it irritating when the tour guide rushes them off.

It’s a challenge to get a good view of artwork when it’s crowded

Since some areas in Gallery have bad connection visitors aren’t able to google information about artwork.

Long booking process and queues are frustrating for users.

Define

What to design?

Pain Points

On the basis of insights collected from the survey and interviews I created 2 personas to portray the user base for this app.

Inadequate information

Interested volunteers are not sure where to start when looking for volunteering opportunities.

Unclear Registration

Long registration processes cause frustration and the tracking of application form is also difficult.

Time & Location

Volunteers want flexibility of location and time to function.

Outdated Information

Most of the websites display outdated information.

Personas

I created two personas to reflect the pain points. These personas helped me to humanize the user throughout the process. It became easier to keep the user in mind before taking any design decision.

Problem Statement

Shriya needs a way to find opportunities which align with her skillset and time because she wants to make a difference.

Goal

My goal is to create a personalized volunteering opportunity searching app which will facilitate both volunteers and NGOs. The application would be a bridge between them.

Reflection

Is it effective?

Impact

The app is instrumental as a bridge between the volunteers and NGOs.It'll facilitate volunteers to find new opportunities easily as well as the NGOs can recruit skilled professions to get the tasks done. User can acheive the satisfaction of contributing towards the society. One quote from peer feedback:

“The filters are amazing and the reminder feature is very helpful. I hope different NGOs come together and develop such an app.”

What I learnt

Initially I believed lack of platform for searching volunteering opportunities was the only problem that needed to be addressed. But throughout the design process I came across various requirements and pain points of users. The solution which I thought was pretty small turned into a complex solution with various parameters to be considered.

Next Steps

Although I had a limited time period to complete the assignments I did my best to focus on the Tour & Booking modules of the app. I would love to work more on this project. Some other features which can be considered for next iterations of the project are:

Including reviews of volunteers on organizations profile and recurring opportunities. Volunteers will be able to find reliable organizations and know about the work culture.

Ability to sign up for a training program if offered by the NGO before volunteering.

Including user’s (volunteer’s) profile which would include their resume( if volunteering for skill based projects) or past volunteer work experience. It would be easier for NGOs to recruit volunteers.

Integrating language options to make it more accessible.

Conducting usability tests for any further iterations.

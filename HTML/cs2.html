PerFit : Clothing Website

Finding the Perfect Fit with VR

Project

Passion Project

My Role

UI/UX Designer

Duration

12 weeks

Overview

Amid the pandemic, Online Shopping has increased rapidly. Although online shopping has drastically changed the way people shop, users still face a number of problems while shopping online. E-commerce websites fail to provide an immersive experience. Their design isn’t optimized to make experience user friendly. On the contrary it confuses user.

With the technological advancement and a better UX design, it’s possible to turn the tables and make the customers happy and satisfied. That’s when I boarded on a journey to a design for a e-commerce website that tries to solve user pain points and make the journey frictionless.

Design Process

I’ve presented the case study in a linear fashion but I follow a lean approach towards designing. Sometimes I brainstorm a few ideas before I’ve conducted user research. It helps me to enter the problem more naturally. Or sometimes even when I’ve completed the research and I’m still prototyping, I constantly look for any new insights from potential users. I believe the process of designing is never ending because there’s no ‘Perfect’ design.

Empathize

What motivates the user?

Online Survey

I sent out the survey form to users from different profession and background. The survey was instrumental in providing insights into user’s preferences and dislikes. The main aim was to collect statistical data regarding user habits or routines regarding shopping. 20 people participated in the survey.

Survey Results

1:1 Interviews

I wanted to understand what elements of in-store shopping do they miss while shopping online and what difficulties they faced. Interviews helped to discover functional requirements from the website. I interviewed 8 people of which 6 were frequent user and 2 were occasional users.

"I only buy online since there’s always so much choice and i t's so relaxing after a hard day at work. I try to read reviews before I buy just to be sure.”

Define

What to design?

Pain Points

I analyzed and synthesized the data found from the user research to define the pain points of users.

Size & Fit Issues

Users aren’t sure about size & fit of the clothes since different brands have different criterion for size.

Return Issues

Users are confused due to lack of clear mention of return & exchange policies.

Navigation

Users get baffled with complex navigation & overwhelmed by the choices.

Checkout

Users need clear checkout process with easy & quick application of their discounts and coupons.

Personas

With all the data collected I created 2 personas. At this stage I had a clear idea of the potential user.

Problem Statement

Vaishali is a busy working professional and a mother who needs well defined filtering and sorting options because she needs to save her time while shopping.

Advet is a busy entrepreneur who needs to help while shopping because he wants to dress appropriately for work.

Goal

My goal was to create a a frictionless shopping experience. I also wanted to make it as personalized as possible. I concentrated on reducing the gap between online and in-store shopping experience.

Test

Is it effective?

Usability Test

Usability Test helped to improve some challenges faced by the participants while using the app.

Key Quotes from Usability Test

“I’m not sure if I would fill all these personal details on the website.”

“Why is the main feature(Virtual Try On) in the corner? Shouldn’t it be highlighted?”

“There are many questions. When will I get the results?”

Key Findings

Participants hesitated to enter their personal information.

Participants got impatient about result after selecting a few options.

Participants thought the information was too cluttered.

Participants mentioned that it was difficult to spot the Virtual Try On Option.

Participants were confused with two Size features.

Iterations

I went back to the designs and made a few iterations in accordance to the key findings from the Usability Test.

Impact

“The Virtual Try On feature was amazing. I can’t wait to use it in real life!!”

What I learnt

I’m an art enthusiast myself which is why I enjoyed working on this case study. Since this was my first case study for the Google UX Design Course, I got the practically explore the field of UX. I got to experience how tightly knitted research and design phase is and how small iterations can have a large impact on people’s life.
